Salmon bowls are the real MVP

They’re packed with protein, omega-3s (hello, brain boost), and all the good fats that keep your skin glowing. Plus, they’re usually loaded with veggies and rice or quinoa, so it’s like a full-on balanced meal that actually slaps. Tasty and good for you? Big win.

SALMON POKE BOWL RECIPE

This fresh and vibrant salmon poke bowl starts with a base of perfectly seasoned sushi rice:

1. Begin by rinsing 1 cup of sushi rice until the water runs clear, then cook according to package instructions.

2. Once cooked, season it with a mix of rice vinegar, sugar, and salt, and let it cool.

3. While the rice is cooling, dice sushi-grade salmon into small cubes and marinate it in soy sauce and sesame oil, with optional sriracha for heat. Let the salmon chill in the fridge for 10–15 minutes.

4. Meanwhile, slice fresh cucumber into thin rounds, dice a ripe avocado, and prepare a scoop of store-bought seaweed salad. You can also sprinkle a little lemon on the avocado to keep it fresh.

5. To assemble the bowl, start with a layer of rice, then arrange the marinated salmon, cucumber slices, avocado chunks, and seaweed salad neatly around it.

6. Finish with a sprinkle of black sesame seeds for that extra pop. Optional toppings like spicy mayo, edamame, or pickled ginger can add extra flavor and flair.

7. Chill the serving bowl beforehand for max freshness. The result? A balanced, nourishing, and photogenic bowl packed with protein, healthy fats, and crunch — ideal for a quick lunch or light dinner that feels like a treat.
